Short summary logp vs narrative length

Methodology

Summary generation

  • Script: experiments/short_summaries_max_chars.py
  • Model: gpt-5.5
  • System prompt (characters limit N=110):
I will give you a narrative below. Summarize it in at most 110 characters per summary (including spaces and punctuation). Each summary should be as informative as possible within that character limit. Give me 10 such short summaries:
The output must be in following JSON format:
```json
{
  "short_summaries": ["short_summary1", "short_summary2", ...]
}
```
  • User message: the story narrative from data/processed/{story}/{story}.json (narrative field)
  • Output: JSON with 10 summaries per story

Log-probability (logp) computation

  • API: Together completions (echo=True, logprobs=1)
  • Model: meta-llama/Llama-3.3-70B-Instruct-Turbo
  • Prompt template:
<|begin_of_text|>{summary_text}
  • Metric: unconditional sum(logp) over summary tokens (no story context)
  • Hypothesis tested below: sum(logp) increases with narrative length
