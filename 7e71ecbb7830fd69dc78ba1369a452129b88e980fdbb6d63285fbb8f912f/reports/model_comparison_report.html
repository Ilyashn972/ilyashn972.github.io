Model Comparison: GPT-4o vs GPT-5.2 for p-caught Correlation

This report summarizes how different models (GPT-4o and GPT-5.2) and different prompts (e.g. emulation vs key points) affect how well generated recalls match human recall patterns, as measured by p-caught correlation with human data.

Main findings (Jan 2026)

Analysis of how models and prompts impact p-caught correlation led to two main conclusions:

  1. Prompt is less important than model. The choice of prompt (e.g. “emulate recall” vs “extract key points”) has less impact on how well the results match human recall patterns than the choice of model.
  2. GPT-4o is more human-like than GPT-5.2. When comparing emulated recalls with the same prompt, GPT-4o produces recall patterns that correlate significantly better with human recall patterns than GPT-5.2.

Temperature and peak recall (Feb 2026)

Follow-up work suggested that GPT-5.2’s lower correlation with human p-caught may be related to temperature: GPT-5.2 does not support temperature control, so its responses are more similar across runs. As a result, the most recalled segments (peaks) tend to be much higher than in human recalls, which can reduce correlation with human p-caught.
